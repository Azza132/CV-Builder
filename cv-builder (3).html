CV Do's & Don'ts
✓ Do
  • ✓ Tailor your CV for every single application
  • ✓ Use bullet points, not paragraphs
  • ✓ Quantify achievements with numbers and %
  • ✓ Mirror keywords from the job description
  • ✓ Keep formatting clean and consistent
  • ✓ Always save and send as a PDF file
  • ✓ Include a LinkedIn profile link
  • ✓ Lead with your strongest, most relevant experience
✕ Don't
  • ✕ Include a photo or date of birth
  • ✕ Add "References available on request"
  • ✕ Use the exact same CV for every job
  • ✕ Write in first person ("I am a...")
  • ✕ Use a generic or unprofessional email address
  • ✕ Include salary expectations or reasons for leaving
  • ✕ Use excessive jargon or vague buzzwords
  • ✕ List every job you've ever had — be selective
Writing Strong Bullet Points
📌 Use the CAR formula — Challenge → Action → Result. Describe the situation, what you did, and the measurable outcome.
🔴 Weak
"Responsible for managing social media accounts and posting content regularly."
🟢 Strong
"Grew Instagram following from 2k to 18k in 6 months by launching a weekly video series, increasing engagement by 140%."
🔴 Weak
"Helped with improving the sales process and worked with the team on projects."
🟢 Strong
"Redesigned the inbound sales workflow, cutting average close time from 21 to 9 days and lifting quarterly revenue by £120k."
Power Action Verbs

Start every bullet point with one of these. Never use "Responsible for" or "Helped with".

The Cover Letter Formula
1
The Hook — Opening Paragraph
Open with genuine enthusiasm. Name the exact role, mention something specific about the company that excites you (their product, mission, a recent launch), and state your single most relevant credential. Never open with "I am writing to apply for..."
2
The Proof — Middle Paragraph(s)
Pick 2–3 achievements from your CV that directly match what the job description asks for. Be specific and use numbers. Connect your past results to what you'd deliver in this new role. Show you've read the job spec carefully.
3
The Close — Final Paragraph
Restate your enthusiasm, thank them for their time, and make a confident call to action: "I would welcome the chance to discuss this further and am happy to arrange a call at your convenience." Sign off warmly but professionally.
More Expert Tips
🔑 Beat the ATS
Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems to filter CVs before a human reads them. Mirror exact phrases from the job description. If they say "stakeholder management", use those words — not "managing relationships".
📏 Length rules
Under 5 years experience: 1 page. 5–15 years: 1–2 pages. Senior/executive: up to 3 pages. Recruiters scan CVs in about 7 seconds initially — make every line earn its place.
📂 File format & naming
Always send as PDF unless asked otherwise. PDFs preserve your formatting on every device. Name your file "FirstName-LastName-CV.pdf" — never "CV-final-v3-FINAL.pdf".
🎯 Tailor every time
A tailored CV is significantly more likely to get an interview than a generic one. Keep a "master CV" with everything, then create a trimmed and reordered version for each specific application.
🕳️ Employment gaps
Don't try to hide gaps — recruiters will notice. Address them briefly and positively. Freelance work, studying, caregiving, or personal development are all legitimate. Be ready to elaborate at interview.
👥 Get a second opinion
Always have someone else proofread your CV and cover letter. You stop seeing your own mistakes after reading the same text too many times. A fresh reader will also spot anything that's unclear or undersells you.
