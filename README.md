# CraftCV — CV & Cover Letter Builder
### Made by Aaron Sermon

---

## What Is CraftCV?

CraftCV is a browser-based tool that helps you build a professional CV and cover letter from scratch. There is no sign-up, no account, and no internet connection required. Everything runs directly in your browser. Fill in your details on the left panel and your documents build in real time on the right.

The app has three sections: CV Builder, Cover Letter, and Tips & Advice.

---

## Getting Started

Open the cv-builder.html file in any modern web browser such as Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. The app will load instantly. You do not need to install anything.

Start by filling in your personal details on the left-hand panel. Your CV will begin to appear on the right as soon as you start typing.

When you are finished, use the Copy Text button to copy your document to your clipboard, or click Print / PDF to save it as a PDF file.

---

## The Left Panel — Your Details

The left panel is where you enter all of your information. It is shared across both the CV Builder and the Cover Letter sections, so you only need to fill it in once.

### Personal Info

Enter your first and last name, job title or professional headline, email address, phone number, location, and LinkedIn or website URL. These will appear at the top of your CV automatically.

### Professional Summary

Write a short 2 to 3 sentence summary of who you are professionally. This will appear just below your contact details on the CV. Keep it concise and focused. Avoid starting with "I am" — write in third person style, for example: "Experienced product designer with 8 years building consumer-facing digital products..."

### Work Experience

Click the Add Role button to add a job. For each role, fill in your job title, company name, dates of employment, and a list of key achievements. Enter each achievement on a separate line. The app will turn them into bullet points automatically on your CV.

You can add as many roles as you need. Click the X button on any role to remove it. To collapse or expand this section, click the Work Experience heading.

### Education

Click the Add Education button to add a qualification. For each entry, fill in your degree or qualification title, the institution name, the dates you attended, and any optional details such as your grade, awards, or relevant modules.

### Skills and Languages

Click inside the skills box and type a skill, then press Enter or comma to add it as a tag. Repeat for as many skills as you need. Do the same for languages. Tags can be removed by clicking the X on each one. These will appear as pill-shaped tags at the bottom of your CV.

### Cover Letter Details

This section feeds into the Cover Letter preview. Fill in the hiring manager's name, the company name, the role you are applying for, and three text areas: your opening paragraph, your key strengths and achievements, and your closing statement. Guidance on what to write in each section can be found in the Tips and Advice tab.

## CV Builder Tab

The CV Builder tab shows a live preview of your CV. It updates instantly as you type in the left panel.

### Choosing a Template

There are three template styles to choose from:

Classic — A clean, traditional layout with a simple ruled header. Suitable for most industries.

Modern — A bold design with a dark header block. Good for creative, tech, or startup roles.

Executive — A refined layout with an accent-coloured name and ruled header. Best for senior or leadership positions.

Click any template card to switch styles. The preview updates immediately.

### Copying and Saving Your CV

Copy Text — Copies the full plain text of your CV to your clipboard. You can paste this into a Word document, Google Doc, or anywhere else.

Print / PDF — Opens your browser's print dialog. To save as a PDF, select Save as PDF from the destination options. This is the recommended way to create a file to send to employers. PDFs preserve your formatting on every device.

## Cover Letter Tab

The Cover Letter tab shows a live preview of your cover letter. It is built from the Cover Letter Details you fill in on the left panel.

The letter automatically includes today's date, a professional salutation, a reference line with the role and company name, your paragraphs, and a sign-off with your name and contact details pulled from the Personal Info section.

If you leave the hiring manager's name blank, the letter will address it to "Dear Hiring Team" by default.

Use the Copy Text button to copy the letter, or Print / PDF to save it.

A golden rule reminder is shown at the top of this tab: every cover letter should be tailored to the specific company and role. Generic letters are far less effective.

## Tips and Advice Tab

The Tips and Advice tab is a full reference guide to help you write the strongest possible CV and cover letter. It does not require any input from you — it is there to read and refer back to.

### CV Checklist

A 12-item checklist covering everything a strong CV should include. Tick each item as you complete it. A progress bar at the bottom tracks how far through the checklist you are. Use this as a final quality check before sending your CV anywhere.

### CV Do's and Don'ts

A side-by-side table of the most important things to do and avoid when writing a CV. Covers common mistakes like including a photo, using first person language, and sending the same CV to every employer.

### Writing Strong Bullet Points

Shows the difference between a weak and a strong bullet point, with real before-and-after examples. Explains the CAR formula: Challenge, Action, Result. Use this to rewrite your experience bullet points before finalising your CV.

### Power Action Verbs

A categorised list of strong action verbs to start your bullet points with. Organised into six groups: Leadership, Growth, Delivery, Innovation, Analysis, and Collaboration. Never start a bullet point with "Responsible for" or "Helped with" — use one of these instead.

### The Cover Letter Formula

A three-step breakdown of how to structure a cover letter:
Step 1 is The Hook — grab attention in the opening paragraph.
Step 2 is The Proof — provide specific evidence that you are the right person for the job.
Step 3 is The Close — end with a confident and professional call to action.

### More Expert Tips

Six additional tip cards covering: how to get your CV past automated screening systems, the correct length for your CV depending on your experience level, how to name and format your file, how to tailor your CV for each application, how to handle employment gaps, and why you should always get a second person to proofread.

## Tips for Best Results

Fill in the job description or role details specifically. The more tailored your content, the better your chances of getting an interview.

Use the checklist before sending your CV anywhere. It covers the most common reasons CVs get rejected.

Read the bullet point examples in the Tips tab before writing your experience section. Strong, quantified bullet points are the single biggest difference between a CV that gets ignored and one that gets an interview.

Always save your final CV as a PDF using the Print / PDF button. Never send a document that could change formatting when opened on a different computer.

Write a new cover letter for every application. Use the Cover Letter Formula in the Tips tab as your guide every time.

## Privacy

CraftCV does not store or send any of your data. Everything you type stays in your browser and is never saved or transmitted anywhere. When you close the tab, your data is gone, so make sure to copy or print your documents before closing.

## Browser Compatibility

CraftCV works in all modern browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. No plugins or extensions are required.
